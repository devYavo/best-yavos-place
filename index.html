I graduated high school in 1997 and just kinda breezed through life with no direction or asperations to try anything. I'm not sure if 'breezed' would be the right way to put it. I've really had to bust my butt to get to where I'm at. But all I wanted to do was hang out with my friends and party. So I always bounced from job to job never knowing what I wanted to do. And since I did not go to college, it made my job choices pretty slim.

Stock boy at a grocery store, fast food, and a clerk at a bait shop before I was 19. Then it was stocking ice cream trucks on overnights until I found my first warehouse job; when I was around 19. Keep in mind, I didn't hold down any of these jobs for more than a year. Nothing felt right. Then a friend of a friend told me I should learn electrical and come work for him. So I kept that for about 2 years and decided that wasn't what I wanted to be doing either.

So there I was, riding the couch because I just didn't know what to do to get me excited to get up in the morning. I couldn't ride it for long, seeing as I had bills to pay and didn't want to be homeless, so I applied at Hell. And even though I hated it, I stuck it out for 7 years until I was able to find another warehouse job that paid a little more. Kept that for about 2 years and life circumstances took me out of state to basically start over.

So I'm in a new area and the only thing I can do is look for warehouse work because that's what is going to pay the most in the area. I kept the first one for a year and a half and couldn't do it anymore. So I tried my luck with electrical again. Held that for 4 years but then Covid hit and I didn't want to go into people's crawlspaces and attics anymore so it was back to warehouse work.

And that's where I'm at today. Another warehouse job that makes me hate waking up in the morning. So I started web development courses at the end of Feb. 2024 and it feels like the thing that I've been missing my whole life. I remember a question they used to ask in school, "If you won the lottery, and didn't have to worry about money, what would you want to do?". I've never had an answer to that question until now.
Just wanted to add that I'm learning web development, not grammer and punctuation. HAHAHAHA!!
